The content engine

3 min read · #content #distribution #utms

The content engine is how you create lots of public-facing “light” content while routing people into your owned pages. The trick is that the blog page stays clean and navigable while the system underneath produces volume.

1) 10x videos/day without losing quality

  • Use a template for the first 2 seconds (hook)
  • Swap only one variable per variant (animal, setting, text overlay)
  • Keep captions short and consistent

2) Captions that behave like mini-landing pages

Each caption should do three things: hook, promise, route. No drama. No essays.

3) Hashtags as indexing, not superstition

Use hashtags like a filing system:

  • 2–3 broad (#viral #shorts #animals)
  • 2 niche (#kitten #bear)
  • 1 brand (#deadapp or #viraldeadengine)

4) The owned-page loop

Short content points to the blog. The blog points to the product. The blog stays usable for humans, but it also quietly supports SEO, retargeting, and attribution.
